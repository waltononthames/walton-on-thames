Most people drive through Hersham on the way to somewhere else. That's their loss.

Just a mile south of Walton-on-Thames town centre, Hersham has preserved a genuine village identity that many Surrey settlements have long since lost to the suburbs. The centrepiece is the village green — a proper English village green with a pond, mature trees, and period houses around the edges.

## The green and pond

The Hersham Village Green is genuinely picturesque. The pond is home to mallards year-round, and the surrounding gardens are well-tended. On a summer evening, with the Barley Mow pub across the road, it's hard to think of a more pleasant spot in the borough.

## Getting around Hersham

What makes Hersham particularly appealing is that it has its own railway station — Hersham, on the Waterloo to Weybridge line — making it a practical base as well as a pleasant one. There are frequent trains to London Waterloo (under 40 minutes) and it's an easy commute.

The station is about a 10-minute walk from the village green, through quiet residential streets.

## A local favourite

Hersham has a loyal community of residents who wouldn't swap it for anywhere else. The local Facebook groups are active and friendly, the village fete (held on the green in summer) is well attended, and there's a strong sense of mutual support that's increasingly rare.

If you've never explored Hersham properly, put it on your list — and allow time to sit outside the Barley Mow with a drink and watch the world pass by on the green.
