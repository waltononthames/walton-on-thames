Walton-on-Thames sits on a picturesque bend of the Thames in north Surrey, a prosperous and well-connected town that manages to feel genuinely local despite its proximity to London (Waterloo in 32 minutes).

The town centre is focused around The Heart shopping centre and the surrounding streets — there's a good range of independent cafes and restaurants alongside the high-street names, and Church Street and High Street have considerable life about them at most times of day. The riverside and Cowey Sale offer some of the finest recreational walking in Surrey, while the rowing clubs, cricket club, and sports grounds give the town a participatory sporting culture you don't find everywhere.

## Character

Walton is comfortable without being pretentious, well-heeled without being ostentatious. It attracts young families priced out of west London as much as long-established Surrey residents; the community mix is genuinely diverse.

## History

The town has been a Thames crossing point for centuries (see [the history of Walton Bridge](/community/news/walton-bridge-history/)). Notable connections include artist Canaletto (who painted the old bridge), actress Julie Andrews (born in nearby Woodham in 1935), and the Hepworth Sculpture Walpole, one of the finest public sculpture collections in the country, housed in a converted pumping station near the riverbank.

## Practical

- **Train:** Walton-on-Thames station (South Western Railway), 32 min to London Waterloo
- **Road:** 2 miles from Junction 11 of the M25
- **Buses:** routes to Weybridge, Esher, Kingston, and Staines
