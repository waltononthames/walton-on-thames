Walton-on-Thames is a riverside town in north-west Surrey, set on the south bank of the River Thames in the Borough of Elmbridge. It is a place with deep parish history, an important Thames crossing, strong rail links to London, a distinctive town centre, active community life and a rich local heritage that includes St Mary's Church, Ashley Park, Walton Bridge and the First World War New Zealand hospital at Mount Felix.

Today Walton-on-Thames is best understood as both a historic Surrey town and a practical modern community — with a railway station on the South Western Railway network, a town centre focused around the High Street and [The Heart](/shopping/the-heart/), riverside open spaces, schools, leisure facilities, and bus links.

## Where is Walton-on-Thames?

Walton-on-Thames is in north-west Surrey, within the Borough of Elmbridge. It sits on the south bank of the River Thames, with Shepperton across the river to the north, Weybridge to the west, Hersham to the south and Esher to the east. The town is close to the edge of Greater London but is not in London — it is part of Surrey and has a strong local identity shaped by the Thames, its parish history, its bridge and its transport connections.

The town's location explains much of its character. Walton is close enough to London to be part of the commuter belt, but its riverside setting, historic church and estate history give it a more layered identity than a simple suburb. The older settlement grew around Church Street, Bridge Street, the parish church and the river, while later growth spread around the railway station, High Street and surrounding residential neighbourhoods.

Nearby places include [Hersham](/neighbourhoods/hersham/), Weybridge, Esher, Shepperton and Molesey.

## Council, constituency and public services

### Local government

Walton-on-Thames is currently served by two councils. **Elmbridge Borough Council** is the borough-level authority, covering planning, waste, housing and local environmental services. **Surrey County Council** is the county-level authority, covering education, highways, social care and fire and rescue.

Walton's electoral geography includes the wards of Walton Central, Walton North and Walton South, though exact ward boundaries and councillor information should always be checked by postcode as these can change.

### Future East Surrey Council

Surrey is undergoing major local government reorganisation. From **1 April 2027**, the current county and district or borough council structure is due to be replaced by two new unitary councils — East Surrey and West Surrey. Walton-on-Thames is expected to fall within **East Surrey**, which will cover the current areas of Elmbridge, Epsom and Ewell, Mole Valley, Reigate and Banstead, and Tandridge.

Until the new arrangements take effect, residents should continue to use Elmbridge Borough Council and Surrey County Council for current services.

### MP and parliamentary constituency

Walton-on-Thames is in the **Esher and Walton** parliamentary constituency. The current MP is **Monica Harding** of the Liberal Democrats, elected in July 2024.

### Police, fire and ambulance

- **Police:** Surrey Police (Elmbridge and Walton area)
- **Fire and rescue:** Surrey Fire and Rescue Service — Walton fire station is on Hersham Road
- **Ambulance:** South East Coast Ambulance Service NHS Foundation Trust, with Walton on Thames Ambulance Station among its operational locations

### NHS services

Walton-on-Thames is part of the **NHS Surrey and Sussex Integrated Care Board** area from 1 April 2026. For acute hospital care, **Ashford and St Peter's Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust** is the main nearby acute provider — St Peter's Hospital is especially relevant to Walton residents, though actual care pathways vary by referral and patient choice.

Mental health services are provided through **Surrey and Borders Partnership NHS Foundation Trust**, with Elmbridge community mental health services being the relevant local route for many adult mental health services. Walton also has local primary care provision including Walton Community Hospital and GP practices.

### Buses

Walton-on-Thames is served by local and regional bus routes within Surrey's wider network. Current verified operators include **Falcon Buses** and **White Bus**, with routes linking Walton with Staines, Shepperton, Esher, Kingston, Weybridge, Hersham, Brooklands, Hatton Cross and St Peter's Hospital. For current timetables, check Surrey County Council's bus pages or Traveline.

## A short history of Walton-on-Thames

Walton-on-Thames has Domesday roots, a medieval parish church, a long Thames crossing history and important estate landscapes — it is a town with considerably more depth than many online summaries suggest.

### Early Walton and Domesday

Walton-on-Thames appears in **Domesday Book in 1086** as *Waletona*. The entry records a church, mills and a fishery, showing that Walton was already an established settlement by the late eleventh century. The name Walton is generally understood as deriving from Old English elements associated with a settlement of the Wealas — a term used by Anglo-Saxons for Britons.

### Cowey Stakes and Julius Caesar

One of Walton's best-known early-history stories is the tradition that Julius Caesar crossed the Thames at **Cowey Stakes**, near the present [Cowey Sale](/visit/things-to-do/cowey-sale/). This is a long-standing local tradition rather than proven historical fact, and should be treated as such — though it is historically interesting precisely because of the debate surrounding it.

### Medieval Walton and St Mary's Church

**St Mary's Church** in Church Street is one of the most important historic buildings in Walton-on-Thames. It is the ancient parish church and is **Grade I listed**, providing a direct link between the modern town and its medieval parish history. The older part of Walton grew around the church, Church Street, Bridge Street and the approach to the river — areas that still retain much of historic Old Walton's character.

### Manors, estates and Ashley Park

Walton's history was shaped by manors and estates as well as by the river. **[Ashley Park](/visit/things-to-do/ashley-park/)** was one of the town's defining estates, associated with prominent local families and civic life before it was broken up and developed. Ashley Park is also connected with one of Walton's more unusual sporting claims: a **1749 reference to a game at Ashley Park** is often cited in baseball history as one of the earliest documentary references to the game — explored in more detail in our [history of baseball in Walton](/community/news/birth-of-baseball-walton-on-thames/).

### Walton Bridge and the Thames crossing

The Thames crossing is central to Walton's story. Before the modern bridge, ferries and earlier bridge structures shaped movement between Walton and Shepperton. The first bridge was built in the eighteenth century, and the site inspired Canaletto's famous paintings of Walton Bridge. The **present Walton Bridge opened in 2013** — its modern design and single-span structure make it one of the town's most recognisable landmarks. Read the full story in our [history of Walton Bridge](/community/news/walton-bridge-history/) and visit the [Walton Bridge attraction page](/visit/things-to-do/walton-bridge/).

### Mount Felix and the New Zealand hospital

**Mount Felix** is one of Walton's most distinctive historical stories. During the First World War, the Mount Felix mansion became a New Zealand military hospital, opening on 31 July 1915, renamed No. 2 New Zealand General Hospital in 1916, and treating around 27,000 men. Most of the mansion has gone, but the **clock tower and former stable block survive and are Grade II listed**, making Mount Felix both a heritage site and a place of international memory connecting Walton with New Zealand's First World War history.

### Railway growth and modern Walton

The arrival of the railway transformed Walton, driving expansion beyond the older parish core and connecting the town to London and the wider south-west suburban rail network. In the twentieth century, Walton became part of Walton and Weybridge Urban District, later replaced by Elmbridge Borough in the 1974 local government reorganisation. More recent change has included the redevelopment of **The Heart**, which brought together retail, housing and public facilities including the library.

For a deeper dive, see our [history and heritage](/visit/history-and-heritage/) section.

## The River Thames and Walton Bridge

The River Thames is central to Walton-on-Thames — it shaped the town's crossing point, landscape, leisure identity and relationship with neighbouring riverside places. [Walton Bridge](/visit/things-to-do/walton-bridge/) is the town's most prominent river landmark, while the Thames towpath provides some of the finest recreational walking in Surrey.

The riverside is one of Walton's most attractive public spaces. **Cowey Sale**, **Riverside Gardens** and the towpath give residents and visitors access to walks, river views and open space, including Elmbridge's Broadwater Lake and Desborough Island circular walk route.

## Historic buildings and landmarks

Walton-on-Thames has lost some important buildings over the years, but retains significant heritage assets:

- **St Mary's Church, Church Street** — Grade I listed, the town's most visible link to its medieval parish
- **Old Manor House, Manor Road** — listed building reflecting Walton's older manorial history
- **Mount Felix clock tower and stable block** — Grade II listed, internationally significant for its First World War New Zealand hospital connection
- **Walton-on-Thames War Memorial** — Grade II listed, standing at a key town-centre junction
- **Walton Church Street and Bridge Street conservation area** — protects the historic core near the river
- **Walton Riverside conservation area** — protects the river setting where Walton's historic character is most visible

## Transport and connections

**Walton-on-Thames railway station** is on the South Western Railway network, providing direct rail connections to London Waterloo (around 32 minutes) and towards Weybridge and the wider south-west rail system. Network Rail completed accessibility improvements at the station in 2024, including a new accessible footbridge, lifts and platform works.

Road connections are shaped by Walton Bridge, with routes towards Weybridge, Hersham, Esher and Shepperton. The M25 (Junction 11) is approximately 2 miles away. Heathrow is the nearest major airport in practical terms.

Bus services connect Walton with nearby towns. Verified routes include connections to Staines, Shepperton, Esher, Kingston, Weybridge, Hersham, Brooklands, Hatton Cross and St Peter's Hospital.

Walton town centre is focused around **The High Street** and **[The Heart](/shopping/the-heart/)** — a major mixed-use development that reshaped the town centre, bringing together retail, housing and public facilities including the library. The town centre functions as a local shopping, services, hospitality and civic centre, with independent cafés and restaurants alongside the high-street names.

## Culture, sport and community life

**Riverhouse Barn Arts Centre** is one of the most important local cultural venues, offering theatre, music, exhibitions, classes and community events throughout the year. The Walton Community Festival and other local events reinforce the town's civic identity.

Sport and leisure are prominent: the **[Elmbridge Xcel Leisure Complex](/directory/elmbridge-xcel/)** offers swimming, fitness, indoor sport and pitches, while Walton also has a strong riverside sporting tradition through Walton Rowing Club.
