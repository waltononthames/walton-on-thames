Hersham occupies a rare position in the Surrey commuter belt: it actually feels like a village. The green is genuine, the pond is genuine, the sense of community is genuine.

A mile south of Walton-on-Thames, Hersham has managed to preserve a distinct identity. The village green and surrounding conservation area give it a character quite different from the more urban feel of Walton town centre, and residents tend to be fiercely fond of the place.

## The village

The Barley Mow pub occupies a prime spot beside the green and is the social hub of the village. The green itself — with its pond, mature trees, and surrounding period architecture — is one of the most photographed views in the borough.

## Beyond the green

Away from the village centre, Hersham is mostly quiet residential streets with good schools and easy access to the green belt. The Mole Valley cycle route passes nearby, and Fieldcommon Farm is a popular local nature walk.

## Getting here

Hersham railway station (South Western Railway) connects to London Waterloo in around 40 minutes. Buses run to Walton-on-Thames and Weybridge. By car it's straightforward from the A244.
