Whiteley Village is unlike anywhere else in Surrey — an octagonal planned settlement set in 225 acres of woodland south of Hersham, built as a model village for retired people of modest means.

The village was funded by the estate of William Whiteley, the Victorian department store magnate known as the "Universal Provider" of Bayswater. His will endowed the construction of a village that would provide comfortable homes for hundreds of elderly residents. The project was a huge undertaking, with eight architects contributing buildings around a central octagonal green, all in a unified Arts and Crafts style.

## Architectural significance

The village is a Registered Historic Park and Garden, and most of its buildings are listed. The central statue of William Whiteley and the domed Temple of Friendship are focal points of the layout. Walking the roads between the cottages feels like stepping back in time.

## Visiting

The village is open for visitors to walk through and appreciate the architecture. It remains an active retirement community, so please be respectful of residents. The woodland paths around the perimeter of the estate are popular with walkers.

Note: Whiteley Village is not on a bus route — a car or taxi from Walton or Hersham is the most practical way to visit.
